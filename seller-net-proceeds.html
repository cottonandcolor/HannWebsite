Understanding the Estimate

What can reduce your seller proceeds?

Your final settlement statement reflects the actual contract, payoff statements, prorations and title-company charges.

01

Mortgage and lien payoff

Your lender’s payoff may include accrued interest, release fees and other charges beyond the principal balance.

02

Broker compensation

Compensation is negotiated in written agreements and should be entered based on the terms being considered.

03

Title and settlement charges

The contract determines who pays the owner’s title policy and other settlement expenses.

04

Tax and HOA prorations

Property taxes, assessments and HOA charges may be prorated or collected at closing.

05

Buyer closing-cost contributions

A seller may agree to contribute toward eligible buyer costs as part of the negotiated contract.

06

Repairs and other expenses

Repair credits, improvements, moving, staging and other transaction-specific costs may affect the final result.

Important Estimate Information

This calculator is provided for general educational and planning purposes only. It is not a settlement statement, appraisal, comparative market analysis, mortgage payoff quote, tax calculation or guarantee of proceeds. Actual costs and proceeds will depend on the sales contract, negotiated broker compensation, lender payoff, title-company charges, property-tax and HOA prorations, repairs, concessions and other transaction-specific expenses. Consult the appropriate real estate, title, lending, tax and legal professionals before relying on an estimate.

Broker compensation is not set by law and is fully negotiable.

Automatic owner’s title-policy estimates use the Texas Department of Insurance basic premium rate schedule effective March 1, 2026. Verify the final premium and applicable credits with the title company.
